What is a leech?

A leech is a visitor who uses another person’s login information to access a restricted area of a website.

cPanel allows you to prevent leeching by redirecting likely offenders or disabling accounts whose passwords have been compromised.

Setup/enable leech protection

1. In the first box, enter the number of logins per username allowed in a 2-hour period.

Note: 4 is the recommended setting.

2. In the second box, enter a URL to redirect leech users to.

Note: Use an URL outside the protected area to avoid an internal server error message.

3. Click the Send Email Alert To box to receive an email when leech protection is triggered. Enter an email address in the appropriate box.

4. Click the Disable Compromised Accounts box to disable user accounts that trigger leech protection.

Note: You must reset the user’s password to re-enable the account.

5. Click Enable.

Disable leech protection

  • Click Disable.

Manage users

  • Click the Manage Users button. You will be directed to the Password Protect Directories feature.
