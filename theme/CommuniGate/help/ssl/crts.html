What is an SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate (CRT)?

An SSL certificate is an electronic document (using the filename extension .crt) which binds a public key to your website. This certificate is necessary for authentication. You can use either a self-signed certificate or a certificate from an authority.

Self-signed certificates are generally reserved for intranet uses. If you plan to use a certificate on the Internet, you should obtain it from a third-party service.

Consult your favorite search engine to shop for SSL providers.

Upload a new SSL certificate

1. Paste the certificate in the Paste the crt below box OR click Browse.

2. Click Upload.

Generate a new SSL certificate

1. Select the appropriate host.

2. Enter your country, state, city, company, company division, and email in the appropriate boxes.

3. Click Generate.

  • Note: You must generate or upload a key before you can generate a certificate.

View or delete a certificate

Certificates installed on your server appear in the Certificates on Server table.

  • To view a certificate, click Show Details.
  • To delete a certificate, click Remove.
